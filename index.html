Dragon

A dragon is a legendary creature, typically described as a large, powerful, serpentine or reptilian beast. The word comes from the Ancient Greek drakōn, meaning "serpent" or "giant seafish.

Wyrm

A wyrm is a type of dragon that is typically portrayed as a giant, limbless, and wingless serpent. It is one of the most primitive and ancient forms of dragons in European folklore, specifically within Germanic and British mythology.

Coatyl

A Coatyl is a feathered serpent with large, rainbow-colored wings. Unlike most dragons or wyrms, which are often bestial or dangerous, the Coatyl is a Celestial being.

Wyvern

A wyvern is a winged, legendary reptile that looks very similar to a dragon but is physically distinct due to its limb structure. While dragons are often portrayed as intelligent and magical, wyverns are usually depicted as animalistic, vicious predators.

Drake

In modern fantasy taxonomy, a Drake is a dragon-like creature that possesses four legs but no wings. While "True Dragons" rule the skies, Drakes rule the ground.
